About Me

I’m Zachary Nicholas, a multidisciplinary problem solver blending mechanical expertise, cybersecurity operations, and backend development into secure, scalable solutions. My journey spans Marine Corps service, academic rigor, technical certifications, and hands-on experimentation — all driven by curiosity and precision.

Military Experience

As a Heavy Equipment Mechanic (MOS 1341) in the United States Marine Corps, I specialized in preventive maintenance and advanced diagnostics on diesel/gas-powered construction machinery. Responsibilities included:

  • Troubleshooting mechanical, hydraulic, and electrical systems
  • Overhauling engines, powertrains, and hydraulic pumps
  • Managing inventory and parts records via GCSS-MC and Excel
  • Supervising junior mechanics and documenting equipment conditions
  • Utilizing technical manuals for accurate task execution
Key Achievements:
  • Streamlined inventory workflows with digital tools
  • Minimized equipment downtime by addressing critical failures promptly
This experience grounded me in technical precision and team leadership — traits I now apply across cybersecurity and systems engineering.

Academic Projects & Technical Evolution

  • Hosted a full-stack web application on AWS to explore cloud deployment
  • Applied NIST SP 800-53 and OWASP best practices in secure development
  • Built embedded systems with TI microcontrollers for calibration tasks
  • Tuned Linux and Windows environments for performance and resilience
  • Created workflows and documentation reflecting real-world operations

These efforts shaped my backend development mindset while reinforcing system protection principles.

Certifications & Professional Development

  • CompTIA: A+, Network+, Security+, CySA+
  • (ISC)²: Certified in Cybersecurity (CC)

Together, they reflect my dedication to structured learning and operational excellence across hardware, networks, and security disciplines.

Ongoing Exploration
